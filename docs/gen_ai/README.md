##### 1. Generative AI (GenAI)

AI systems that create new content such as text, images, audio, video, or code based on patterns learned from training data.

Examples: ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini

##### 2. Large Language Models (LLMs)

Models trained on massive a mounts of text to understand and generate human-like language.

Examples: GPT-5, Llama

##### 3. Tokens

LLMs process text as tokens rather than words. A token may be a word, part of a word, or punctuation.

Example:

"Generative AI is amazing" → several tokens depending on the tokenizer.

##### 4. Prompt Engineering

The practice of designing effective instructions to get better outputs from AI models.

Techniques:

Clear instructions
Role prompting
Step-by-step prompting
Output formatting

Example:

"Act as a data scientist and explain neural networks with examples."

##### 5. Context Window

The amount of information an AI model can consider at one time, including prompts, conversation history, and uploaded content.

##### 6. Embeddings

Numerical representations of text, images, or other data that capture meaning and relationships.

Uses:

Semantic search
Recommendations
Similarity matching

##### 7. Vector Database

A database optimized for storing and searching embeddings.

Popular examples: Pinecone, Weaviate, Milvus

##### 8. Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)

A technique where an LLM retrieves relevant information from external knowledge sources before generating a response.

Benefits:

- More accurate answers
- Access to up-to-date information
- Reduced hallucinations

##### 9. Fine-Tuning

Further training a pre-trained model on domain-specific data to improve performance for a particular task.

Examples: Medical chatbot, Legal assistant, Customer support bot

##### 10. Hallucination

    When an AI generates information that sounds correct but is inaccurate or fabricated.

    Mitigation:

    RAG
    Fact-checking
    Human review

##### 11. Transformer Architecture

    The foundational neural network architecture behind most modern LLMs.

    Key innovation:

    Attention mechanism

##### 12. Attention Mechanism

    Allows a model to focus on the most relevant parts of the input when generating output.

##### 13. Temperature

    A parameter controlling randomness in generated responses.

    Low temperature (0–0.3): More deterministic
    Medium (0.5–0.8): Balanced
    High (1.0+): More creative

##### 14. Zero-Shot, One-Shot, and Few-Shot Learning

    Zero-shot: No examples provided.
    One-shot: One example provided.
    Few-shot: A few examples provided.

##### 15. Agents

    AI systems that can:
    Reason
    Use tools
    Access databases
    Execute actions
    Complete multi-step tasks

    Examples include customer-service agents and coding assistants.

##### 16. Multimodal AI

    Models that can process multiple data types:

    Text
    Images
    Audio
    Video

    Examples: GPT-4o, Gemini

##### 17. AI Evaluation Metrics

    Common measures include:

    Accuracy
    Precision
    Recall
    F1 Score
    BLEU
    ROUGE

##### 18. AI Safety and Alignment

    Ensuring AI systems behave according to human goals and ethical principles.

    Topics:

    Bias mitigation
    Fairness
    Privacy
    Security
    Responsible AI

##### 19. Model Training Pipeline

    Data Collection
    Data Cleaning
    Pre-training
    Fine-tuning
    Evaluation
    Deployment
    Monitoring

##### 20. MCP (Model Context Protocol)

    A protocol that allows AI models to connect to external tools, databases, APIs and applications in a standardized way.
